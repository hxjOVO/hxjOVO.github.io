About me

Mechanistic RNA biology with cross-disciplinary training.

I am a Postdoctoral Research Scientist at Columbia University Irving Medical Center. My research focuses on the molecular mechanisms that regulate RNA processing and small RNA maturation, especially the Lin28/let-7 pathway and terminal uridylyltransferase activity.

During my Ph.D. training in Medical Sciences at The University of Tokyo, I investigated Lin28-dependent pre-let-7 oligo-uridylylation by TUT4. This work deepened my interest in how RNA-binding proteins and RNA-modifying enzymes coordinate specificity, timing, and biological outcome.

My earlier training in natural product chemistry and enzymology at the University of Toyama adds a complementary chemical biology perspective. I enjoy working across molecular biology, structural thinking, enzymatic mechanisms, and data-driven approaches to answer precise biological questions.
